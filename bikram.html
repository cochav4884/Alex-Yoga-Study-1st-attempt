Bikram

Bikram-distinct 26 postures that is practiced in a room that is 105 degrees Farenheit. Made for people who has sustained a big injury and needs rehabilitation. People who practice Bikram yoga believe that the heat helps warm and calm the body before the 26 poses are taken.

26 postures: standing deep breathing, half moon pose, awkward pose, eagle pose, standing head to knee, standing bow, balancing stick, standing separate leg stretching pose, triangle pose, standing leg head to knee pose, tree pose, toe stand, dead body, wind-removing pose, sit up, cobra pose, locust pose, full locust pose, bow pose, fixed firm pose, half-tortoise pose, camel pose, rabbit pose, head to knee (stretching pose), spine-twisting pose, blowing in firm pose

Health Benefits of Bikram/hot yoga -flush out all the bad toxins in your body and in your skin -helps relieve inner stress and builds confidence within oneself -it is a practice of "mind over matter", making sure that you can get through the whole practice without passing out or giving up -get a sense of self empowerment

What is the purpose of it? -many athletes tend to like hot yoga better than traditional yoga because it can really help recover from inury -the founder of Bikram yoga started doing it because he suffered from an injury, so therefore, many people who have sustained a big injury practice Bikram to help them recover faster -after going to one or two classes, you start to feel better about yourself and your body -definitely would recommend this type of class to someone who is interested in yoga
